SeikakuBot Trading Strategy

Before trading, it’s very important to read this information!

1. General principle of the strategy:

1. The strategy analyzes price data and indicators (RSI, Stoch, MACD, ATR, etc.) on 15-minute and 1-hour timeframes.

2. A machine learning model predicts the probability of a price increase (prob_up). If the probability is high enough, the strategy suggests a BUY signal; if it’s low, a SELL signal.

3. For each signal, Stop-Loss (SL) and Take-Profit (TP) are calculated based on volatility (ATR). This is done to limit losses and secure profits when the target price is reached.

2. How to enter a trade based on the signal

1. Receiving the signal

When the bot sends a notification for a possible trade (with specified Entry, SL, and TP), you will see:

  • Entry (Entry price) – the intended price to enter the trade.
  • Stop-Loss (Stop Loss) – the level at which to close the trade if the price moves unfavorably.
  • Take-Profit (Take Profit) – the level at which to lock in profit once the price target is reached.
  • Type of trade (BUY or SELL) – going long (buy) or going short (sell).

2. Opening the trade

  • If the signal is BUY, place a buy order around the Entry price.
  • If the signal is SELL, place a sell (short) order around the Entry price.

Note: On a real market, there might be slippage, so the actual entry price may slightly differ from the “ideal” figures.

3. Stop-Loss and Take-Profit

  • Right after opening a position, set the Stop-Loss (SL) and Take-Profit (TP) as indicated by the bot.
  • This prevents excessive losses if the market goes against you, and ensures profit is taken if the price reaches the target.

3. Risk management and position sizing

1. Recommended risk per trade – no more than 1–2% of your total deposit.

Example: If you have 1000 USDT in your account, risk no more than 10–20 USDT (1–2% of 1000 USDT) on each trade.

This approach allows you to withstand a series of losing trades without a catastrophic drop in your account balance.

2. Why 1–2% specifically?

The market can be unpredictable, and losing trades are a normal part of any trading system.

Trading with a small risk per trade helps maintain calm and discipline, allowing the strategy to work over the long term.

3. Calculating trade volume

If a signal indicates a risk/reward ratio of about 1:1.5 or 1:2, that’s enough for a series of winning trades to cover a series of losing ones.

The key is to strictly adhere to your risk limits, to avoid “riding out” losses, and not to open oversized positions.

4. Why the strategy can work even with < 50% winning trades

In the reports, you might notice that the Win Rate (the percentage of winning trades) can be around 45–50%.

January 2025 reports:

  • Total trades: 7362
  • Win Rate: 45.69%
  • Final return: 495.64%
  • Maximum drawdown: 67.30%

Despite that, the final return can still be high thanks to proper risk management and a positive “average profit / average loss” ratio.

In other words, when a trade is profitable, the profit is usually larger than the loss in a losing trade. This compensates for having only about half (or even slightly fewer) winning trades.

5. Additional tips

1. Never move the set Stop-Loss. Moving the SL “further away,” hoping the market will turn around, is the quickest way to incur large losses.

2. Pay attention to liquidity and volatility. In a calm market, signals may take longer to play out; in a volatile market, they may execute quickly.

3. Regularly withdraw some profit (if trading on a live account). This helps lock in gains and offers psychological support.

4. Always remember that the market does not guarantee profit. Any strategy can go through a losing streak, and you must be prepared both financially and mentally.

6. Conclusion

The strategy is based on a mathematical model and statistics. It does not provide a 100% guarantee, but it can perform over the long run through strict adherence to entry, risk, and exit rules.

The key to success is discipline. Even the best strategy can turn unprofitable if you don’t follow the prescribed risk and respect your Stop-Loss.

Beginners are advised to practice on a demo account or trade with minimal volumes until they are comfortable following the system.

Happy trading! And remember, no strategy is immune to all market risks, so always trade responsibly and within your own risk tolerance.
